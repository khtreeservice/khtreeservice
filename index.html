Services Overview

As a Graduate from the Sir Sandford Fleming College Forestry Technician program I have studied tree health and proper pruning practices for optimizing the healing of wounds on trees whether made by nature or human interference. While it is important to know which cuts to make, it is just as important to know which ones not to make and how not to make them. When a tree can be maintained it has a much better chance of providing the benefits trees do while reducing the risk a failing tree has to people and property.
Maintaining your forested trails is an important part of ensuring safety while enjoying the trails and promoting specific, native forest species that will thrive in the conditions present. Knowing the type of forest your trails traverse is an important part of helping them reach their potential.
While I prefer the idea of saving trees I understand some trees just cannot be saved. For tree removal the following options can be considered:
Drop and leave material.
Bucking for firewood.
On-Site chipping of brush.
Partial removal of material.
Total removal of material.
